Direct Filing Addresses for Form I-131, Application for Travel Document

Use the chart below to determine where you must file your Form I-131, Application for Travel Document, if you are not filing online via myUSCIS.gov.

Applications that are not submitted to the appropriate direct filing address may experience processing delays.

Applicants with a pending Form I-485
If you are… Send your form to the…

Filing your Form I-131 together with your Form I-485

 Same address that you are filing your Form I-485. Go to our Direct Filing Addresses for Form I-485, Application to Register Permanent Residence or Adjust Status page for the current information on where to file both forms.

Filing your Form I-131 alone, and your Form I-485 receipt notice (Form I-797C, Notice of Action) begins with:

  • MSC (example: MSC1234567890);
  • IOE (example: IOE1234567890); or
  • Does not have three letters at the beginning (example: 1234567890).

Go to our USCIS Lockbox Filing Locations Chart for Certain Family-Based Forms page for the filing address based on where you live.

Filing your Form I-131 alone, and your Form I-485 receipt notice (Form I-797C, Notice of Action) begins with:

  • EAC (example: EAC123054956); or
  • SRC (example: SRC0595784321).

An Afghan national inside the United States applying for Re-Parole under Operation Allies Welcome (OAW): 

  • Select 1.e. in Part 2 of Form I-131;
  • Handwrite “REPAROLE” at the top of the form;
  • Enter “OAR” or “PAR” as Class of Admission;
  • If you are also requesting an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) upon approval of a new period of parole:
    • And you are using the 06/06/23 E or the 06/06/23 edition of Form I-131, select “Yes” in Part 8. Do not submit a Form I-765.
    • And you are using the 10/31/22 edition of Form I-131, also handwrite “OAW EAD” at the top of the form, regardless of whether your Class of Admission is PAR or OAR.  Do not submit a Form I-765.

Refugee travel document applicants
If you are: Send your form to:
  • Currently inside the United States:
    • You should apply for a refugee travel document and appear for any required biometrics service appointment before you leave the United States.
 Go to our USCIS Lockbox Filing Locations Chart for Certain Employment-Based Forms page for the filing address based on where you live.
  • Currently outside the United States:
    • You may file Form I-131 and apply for a refugee travel document if you have been outside the United States for less than one year at the time of filing.
    • Although we have discretion to accept your Form I-131 if you file it after you have left the United States, we will not automatically accept or approve your application.

Parole in place (inside the U.S.) for service members and their family
If you are… Send your form to…

 

Inside the United States and applying for parole in place and you are an active duty member of the U.S. armed forces, or a member of the Selected Reserve of the Ready Reserve

The USCIS field office that has jurisdiction over where you live. If you are currently on assignment away from your permanent residence, you may file your Form I-131 at either the office with jurisdiction over where you currently live or over your permanent residence.

You may also submit your application in person at your local USCIS field office.

Inside the United States and applying for parole in place as the spouse, widow(er), parent, son, or daughter, of an active duty member of the U.S. armed forces, a member of the Selected Reserve of the Ready Reserve, or an individual (whether still living or deceased) who previously served on active duty or in the Selected Reserve of the Ready Reserve and was not dishonorably discharged

In the case of a parent of a service member, the service member must support the parent’s request for parole in place

The USCIS field office that has jurisdiction over where you live. If you are currently away from your permanent residence due to the service member being away on assignment, you may file your Form I-131 at either the office with jurisdiction over where you currently live or your permanent residence.

You may also submit your application in person at your local USCIS field office.
Parole Document for current and former service members outside of the U.S.

We generally send parole requests filed by individuals currently in removal proceedings or previously removed from the United States to U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) for review.
